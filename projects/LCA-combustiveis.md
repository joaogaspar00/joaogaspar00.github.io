## Overview

Comparative Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) of conventional Jet-A1 kerosene and two sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) pathways — Fischer–Tropsch synthetic kerosene and HEFA (Hydroprocessed Esters and Fatty Acids) bio-jet — evaluated across their full cradle-to-wake life cycles. The study quantified greenhouse gas emissions, energy demand, and key environmental impact categories for each fuel.

## Methodology

- System boundary defined from feedstock extraction/cultivation through refining, distribution, and combustion.
- Inventory data sourced from the Ecoinvent database and published CORSIA lifecycle methodology.
- Impact assessment performed with the ReCiPe 2016 midpoint method covering global warming potential (GWP100), fossil depletion, and acidification.
- Sensitivity analysis on allocation method (mass, energy, economic) carried out to bound uncertainty.

## Key Results

- HEFA bio-jet delivered a **65 % reduction in GWP100** versus Jet-A1 on a per-MJ basis under favourable feedstock assumptions.
- Fischer–Tropsch pathway showed 50–60 % GWP reduction but higher land-use impact compared to HEFA.
- Sensitivity analysis revealed allocation choice as the dominant source of variability in the bio-jet pathways.
